Month 1 milestone — "I am a Webflow Developer"
By end of this month you can say: I understand HTML/CSS logic visually, I can build any responsive multi-page site from scratch, I understand CMS collections, and I can add scroll-triggered animations. You are not fast yet. You are not polished yet. But you can build.
Week 1 — Days 1–7 — HTML/CSS visual logic. The hardest week of the entire journey.
Day 1What is Webflow? Divs, classes, the box model▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — "Webflow 101 Crash Course" Part 1
  • Go to university.webflow.com. Find "Webflow 101 Crash Course" playlist.
  • Watch: "Intro to HTML & CSS", "Div blocks and classes", "The box model". Pause every 2–3 minutes and write what you learned in a notebook.
  • One rule: if you don't understand something, rewind and rewatch. Never move forward confused.
→university.webflow.com → "Webflow 101 Crash Course"
6pm – 10pm · BUILDRecreate exactly what you watched. From zero.
  • Open Webflow. Create a new blank project.
  • Build what was shown in today's videos without looking at them. If stuck — look back, fix it, close the video again.
  • Goal: one div structure with a heading, paragraph, and a button. Styled with a class. Responsive on mobile.
It will look terrible. That is correct. Your job today is to understand the concept, not make something beautiful.
Day 2Flexbox — how modern layouts are built▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — Flexbox lessons
  • Watch all flexbox videos in the 101 course. Flexbox is how almost every Webflow layout works. This is critical.
  • Concepts to nail: flex direction, justify content, align items, flex wrap, gap. Write each one down with a drawing of what it does.
  • Also watch: "Nested divs" lesson.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDBuild a 3-column card layout using only flexbox
  • Three cards side by side on desktop. Stacked vertically on mobile. No template, no copying — just what you watched today.
  • Each card has: an image placeholder (colored div), a title, short text, a button.
  • Spend the full 4 hours. Go slowly. Fix every alignment issue.
Day 3CSS Grid + spacing system (padding, margin)▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — Grid + spacing videos
  • Watch: "CSS Grid in Webflow", "Spacing and sizing". Also watch the "Typography" video — font size, line height, font weight.
  • Key insight for grid: when to use flexbox vs grid. Grid = 2D layouts (rows AND columns). Flexbox = 1D (one direction).
6pm – 10pm · BUILDRebuild yesterday's card layout using Grid instead of Flexbox
  • Same 3-card layout but built with CSS Grid this time. Understand the difference by feeling it.
  • Then add: a proper heading above the cards with good spacing. Use the spacing concepts from today's lesson.
  • Add proper typography — different font sizes for heading vs body text. Use Google Fonts — import one font.
Day 4Navbar + hero section — the most common things you'll build▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — Navbar and symbols/components
  • Watch: "Navigation bars in Webflow", "Webflow components (symbols)".
  • A navbar is on every site you'll ever build. Learn it thoroughly. Hamburger menu on mobile, dropdown links, logo placement.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDBuild a full hero section with navbar
  • Navbar: logo placeholder (text), 3 nav links, a CTA button. Must collapse to hamburger on mobile.
  • Hero section: big headline (try 72px+), subheadline, two buttons (primary + secondary style), a background color or image placeholder.
  • This single combination — navbar + hero — appears in 100% of client projects. Know it cold.
Day 5Responsive design — breakpoints, mobile-first▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — full responsive design series
  • Watch every video in the "Responsive design" section. All of them.
  • Understand: changes flow DOWN breakpoints (desktop → tablet → mobile). Never UP. This trips every beginner.
  • Learn: min-width, max-width, auto sizing, overflow hidden.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDMake your day 4 hero + navbar fully responsive
  • Open your day 4 project. Switch to tablet view. Fix everything that breaks. Then mobile landscape. Then mobile portrait.
  • Nothing should overflow. Text should be readable. Buttons should be tappable size (minimum 44px height).
  • This is frustrating work. Stay with it. Responsive design is what clients pay for.
Day 6Positioning, z-index, overflow — the "why does it break" concepts▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — positioning videos + YouTube supplement
  • Watch: "Position in Webflow" (static, relative, absolute, fixed, sticky).
  • YouTube: search "Webflow position absolute tutorial" — watch one 15-min video from Flux Academy on this topic. Absolute positioning inside relative parents is used constantly for overlapping elements.
  • Watch: "Overflow in Webflow". Understand hidden vs visible vs scroll.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDBuild a "floating badge" card and a sticky header
  • Card with an absolutely positioned badge (like "New" or "Popular") in the top-right corner of the card.
  • Then make your navbar from day 4 sticky — it should stay at top while scrolling.
  • These two techniques appear in almost every premium site.
Day 7Week 1 project — build a full landing page from scratch▼
10am – 2pm · BUILDBuild sessions 1: structure and layout
  • Build a full 1-page website for a fake company ("TechFlow SaaS" or any name). Sections: Navbar, Hero, Features (3 cards), Testimonial, Simple footer.
  • No looking at tutorials today. Use only what you know. If you forget how to do something, that's fine — figure it out.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDBuild session 2: styling and responsive
  • Continue the same project. Make it fully responsive. Add a color palette (choose 1 accent color + white + dark). Apply consistent typography.
  • By 10pm this should be a complete, published Webflow site. Go to webflow.com/dashboard → publish on free subdomain. Share the link with no one yet — just see it live.
This is your week 1 checkpoint. It will look basic. That is fine. You built a real website from scratch in 7 days starting from zero. That is real progress.

Week 3 — Days 15–21 — Interactions and Animations. The exciting week.
Day 15Interactions overview — triggers, animations, timelines▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — Interactions series from start
  • Watch: "Intro to Interactions", "Page load animations", "Mouse/hover animations". Every video in the interactions series. All of it today.
  • Key concepts: triggers (page load, scroll into view, hover, click), actions (move, fade, scale, rotate), timing (duration, delay, easing).
  • Watch the easing curves carefully. The difference between a cheap animation and a premium animation is almost entirely in the easing curve.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDAdd 5 different animations to your agency site
  • 1. Page load: hero heading fades up on page load (opacity 0→1, move up 30px, 0.6s ease out).
  • 2. Scroll trigger: each features card fades in with a stagger (0.1s between each card).
  • 3. Hover: button background color transition + slight scale.
  • 4. Hover: nav links underline draw animation.
  • 5. Scroll trigger: counter numbers animate up (use Webflow interactions + a number element).
Day 16Scroll animations — the thing clients actually pay premium for▼
10am – 2pm · LEARNWebflow University — ScrollTrigger + GSAP visual integration
  • Watch: "Scroll-based animations in Webflow", "GSAP in Webflow (visual, no code)". As of late 2025 Webflow has GSAP built in visually — you don't need to write code.
  • YouTube: search "Webflow scroll animation 2025 Flux Academy" — watch 1 video (20–30 min).
  • Learn: scrub animations (tied to scroll position), pin elements, parallax effects.
6pm – 10pm · BUILDBuild a scroll-driven section from scratch
  • A section where text slides in from the left as you scroll down to it.
  • A parallax hero background (image moves slower than scroll).
  • A "sticky" section where a heading stays pinned while content scrolls past it. This effect appears on Apple product pages and costs clients $2k+ when done right.
Days 17–19Clone 1 premium Awwwards site — your most important 3 days so far▼
10am – 2pm + 6pm – 10pm · CLONE3 full days: pick 1 site from Awwwards and clone it
  • Go to awwwards.com. Find a "Site of the Day" from the last 3 months. Pick one that has scroll animations but isn't 3D (no Spline yet). Good example types: SaaS landing page, creative agency, product marketing page.
  • Day 17: Clone the navbar and hero section only. Don't rush. Get every pixel right.
  • Day 18: Clone the next 3–4 sections. Focus on matching the animations.
  • Day 19: Clone the footer and do full responsive. Publish it.
You will spend entire hours on one animation. This is normal. Cloning is the single fastest way to build professional-level skill. Every hour here is worth 3 hours of tutorial watching.
