🇧🇩 The best news in this entire conversation
Your IT freelance income is 100% tax-free right now
The Bangladesh Finance Act 2024 amended Schedule 6 of the Income Tax Act 2023 to confirm: income from IT/tech-based services and freelancing earned from abroad is 100% exempt from income tax for the period July 1, 2024 to June 30, 2027. Webflow development, web design, and digital services all qualify. Your entire net income after platform cuts and tool costs goes directly into your pocket.
📋 What you still need to do (even with zero tax)
📌 Get a TIN: Free, takes 10 minutes at etaxnbr.gov.bd. Required for bank accounts, Payoneer withdrawal, and official processes.

📌 File a return annually: Even if you owe ৳0, file by November 30 each year. It's a zero-tax return. Without it you can't renew passport or open bank accounts.

📌 Route money through official banking: The exemption applies only to income remitted through official banking channels (Payoneer → Bangladesh bank, Wise → bank).
📅 What happens after June 2027?
The exemption ends June 30, 2027. By then you should be earning significantly. At $1,000–$2,000/mo you'd be in the 5–10% bracket after threshold. Very manageable — and the government has historically extended IT exemptions. Watch for the Finance Act 2027 announcement.
🧾 VAT situation
VAT is not applicable on freelance services exported to foreign clients. The NBR classifies this as zero-rated export of services. You do not collect or pay VAT on Fiverr or Upwork income from international clients.
⚠️ Important disclaimer: Tax laws in Bangladesh change every fiscal year. I'm not a tax professional. This is based on the Finance Act 2024 and current NBR policy. Always verify with a local CA (Chartered Accountant) before filing — about ৳2,000–৳5,000, worth every taka once your income crosses $500/mo regularly.
